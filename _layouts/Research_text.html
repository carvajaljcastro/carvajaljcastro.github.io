Evolution of Parental Care in Frogs

As part of my Ph.D thesis, I studied how the coloration (i.e., aposematism) increased and promoted the diversity and complexity of parental care behaviors in poison frogs. Additionally, we have explored the relationship between predation risk and tadpoles transport in a species frog using trained chickens and images of frogs.

Species Distribution Models and Climate Change

For one of my thesis chapter, I am construncting the species distribution models for many frog species using physiological and behavioral data. My idea is test the potential consequences of climate change in species distribution and what we can contribute for species conservation.
